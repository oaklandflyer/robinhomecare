Care tailored to the life already being lived.

From a weekly check-in to round-the-clock support, our services flex around your family's actual routine. Here's what we offer, and what it looks like in practice.

01

Personal care

The day-to-day support that keeps independence intact: washing, dressing, mobility, meals, and the morning and evening routines that anchor a good day. Delivered by the same small team so privacy and preferences are respected.

  • Bathing, showering, and continence support
  • Dressing, grooming, and hair care
  • Mobility and transfers around the home
  • Meal preparation and feeding support
  • Light housekeeping alongside care visits
02

Companionship

For older adults living alone, the right companion can be the difference between a week that drifts and a week that feels lived in. A cup of tea, a walk to the park, a crossword, a trip to the library – we match carers who genuinely enjoy the company.

  • Regular visits from a familiar carer
  • Accompanied outings, walks, and appointments
  • Shared hobbies: reading, gardening, music
  • Support with correspondence and phone calls
  • A watchful eye for subtle changes in wellbeing
03

Live-in care

For families who want to avoid a residential move, a dedicated live-in carer provides continuous presence in the home. Routine, privacy, pets, and the garden all stay – and so do you.

  • 24-hour on-site support from a matched carer
  • Personal care, cooking, and household routines
  • Regular respite cover built into the rota
  • Overnight support for restlessness or medication
  • A genuine alternative to a care home
04

Dementia support

Dementia care asks for patience, imagination, and steady routine. Our dementia-trained carers focus on what remains rather than what has changed, and we work closely with families to keep life as recognisable as possible.

  • Dementia-specialist carer matching
  • Validation-based communication techniques
  • Routine-building and environmental cues
  • Family coaching and respite planning
  • Coordination with GPs and memory clinics
05

Medication management

Safe, consistent medication support with clear written records so every family member and every visiting professional knows where things stand. We work with local pharmacies to simplify repeats and reduce errors.

  • Prompting, administering, and recording doses
  • Blister-pack and MAR chart management
  • Liaison with GP and pharmacy for repeats
  • Observation for side-effects and changes
  • Secure medication storage protocols
06

Respite & short-term care

If you're the main carer, you need time too. Whether it's a weekend away, a holiday abroad, or cover after a hospital stay, we step in with familiar faces so the person you care for barely notices the change in routine.

  • Planned respite from a few hours to several weeks
  • Hospital discharge and reablement support
  • Emergency cover for family carers
  • Overnight sitting services
  • Short-term post-operative recovery care
How it works

From first call to first visit, in four clear steps.

We keep the process simple and personal. No lengthy forms online, no faceless call centres – just a real conversation, a visit, and a plan.

01

A conversation

Call or message us. We'll listen, ask a few questions, and arrange a free visit at a time that suits you.

02

A home assessment

Our care coordinator visits, meets the family, and writes up a plan around the person's actual routine – not a template.

03

Carer matching

We introduce two or three potential carers. You choose the ones that feel right. Continuity is the whole point.

04

Care begins

Visits start, we stay in close touch for the first fortnight, and adjust anything that isn't quite right.
